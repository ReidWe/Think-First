A Call to Syracuse University

The Blank Page First.

Think first. Refine later.

The Core Issue

The blank page is disappearing, and so is the struggle that makes us learn.

Cognitive Outsourcing

When students use AI to skip the struggle of idea generation, they miss out on building creative confidence. Intellectual challenges spark motivation, but AI bypasses this process (Hasan, 2025). Brainstorming feels easier, but only because the AI is doing the thinking for them (Habib et al., 2024).

The Pattern in Our Data

We found that 47% of surveyed students introduce AI during prewriting or drafting—the most critical stages for developing original thought. Students who use AI "always" or "often" reported lower confidence in their own creativity (6/10), while those who "rarely" use it reported high confidence (9-10/10). This suggests that relying on AI too early may hurt creative self-confidence.

Inconsistent Guidance Makes It Worse

Inconsistent AI policies make this problem worse. Without standard AI literacy programs, students are left unprepared (Araujo & Schneider, 2025). Policy clarity varies wildly across different colleges on campus. In this confusing environment, students naturally default to the easiest path.

So universities banned it. That made it worse.

In one Syracuse coding class with a strict blanket ban, a professor had to file 37 academic integrity reports in a single semester. Banning AI does not stop usage. It criminalizes it.

Relying too heavily on AI hurts independent problem-solving (Kim et al., 2025), but banning it only drives usage underground. The answer isn't prohibition. It is providing clear structure on when and how to use the tool.

Syracuse University Survey Data

We asked. Students answered.

We surveyed 15 Syracuse University students across 8 different schools to understand how they use AI, how confident they feel without it, and how clear they find their university's policies.

Demand the blank page.

The stakes extend well beyond a single writing class. If students outsource their initial thinking to AI throughout college, they carry that habit into their professional lives. Higher education is built on cultivating original thinkers. As tools to bypass original thought become more advanced, this mission becomes more urgent than ever.

Talk to your professors. Email your dean. Ask Syracuse to implement the Think First framework, so every student confronts the blank page before opening a chatbot.
